Apex Legends Mobile Lost Its Last Chance as Respawn Shuts Down Key Projects

By Alex | 30 April 2025

Apex Legends Mobile is officially out of the game for good. Respawn Entertainment has confirmed the cancellation of two early-stage projects — including one directly tied to Apex’s mobile future. This marks the end of any possibility of revival for the once-promising title.

The Final Nail in the Coffin

After being removed from app stores in 2023, many fans hoped Apex Legends Mobile might return in a new form. However, Respawn's recent restructuring has erased those hopes. In an official statement, the studio confirmed the termination of an internal mobile project:

Respawn: “We’ve made the difficult decision to stop development on two early-stage projects. One of these was related to the Apex Legends universe but did not meet our quality expectations or long-term vision.”

Why the Mobile Game Fell Apart

  • Inconsistent Content Drops: Players were frustrated by slow updates and a lack of fresh content.
  • Technical & Performance Issues: From device optimization problems to server lag, technical flaws plagued the experience.
  • Overly Aggressive Monetization: The in-game economy leaned too heavily on microtransactions, alienating many users.

Respawn Refocuses on Core Projects

Rather than stretching itself thin, Respawn is now focused on strengthening the core Apex Legends experience on console and PC. The cancelled mobile project reportedly failed to meet internal expectations, prompting the team to reallocate its efforts.

No Comeback on the Horizon

With the cancellation confirmed, Apex Legends Mobile won’t be returning — not as a reboot, not as a spin-off. EA and Respawn have officially moved on. Mobile FPS players will now turn to competitors like Warzone Mobile, PUBG Mobile, and Call of Duty: Mobile to fill the void.
